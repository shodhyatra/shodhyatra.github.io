41st Shodh Yatra to Sikkim

The Group

The 41st Shodh Yatra to Tashiding, Sikkim was conducted on Dec 12-14 under the leadership of Prof. Anil K Gupta who was assisted by Ms. Gauri Paliwal. The Shodh Yatri group of 27 students of IIM Ahmedabad comprised of students from Germany, Norway, France, Italy and Tanzania along with Indian students. The group was guided by 12 volunteers headed by Mr. Tshering Lepcha.

The Journey

Shodh Yatra is an attempt to reach out to the remotest part of the country with a firm belief that hardship and the challenges of natural surroundings are the prime motivators of creativity and innovation. Shodh Yatra aims at unearthing such traditional knowledge and grassroots innovations that have not only simplified the lives of men, women and farm labourers but have also significantly contributed towards the conservation of biodiversity.

Shodh Yatra to Tashiding,Sikkim started from Siliguri in west- Bengal were the group met for the first time. The 110 km drive to tashiding took almost the entire day owing to rugged and mountainous terrain. The actual yatra on foot began the next day with the group meeting members from the community at the village administration centre and schools and halting for the night at a community stay. Throughout these meetings, problems faced by the community spanning social,economic and political spheres were discussed. Brainstorming sessions were also conducted at these spots to draw out ideas that the community could benefit from. The subsequent days of the yatra took the group through buddhist monasteries and free flowing rivers were the yatris engaged in silent sessions to disengage from the frenetic pace of life. The final leg of the Yatra ended at the home of a village head where the participants recommended solutions to the improve and leverage the potential of tourism in India.
